Getting Started

First Time Setup

When you first launch Jamber3, you'll need to scan your music collection:

  1. Click the Settings ‚öôÔ∏è button
  2. Add your music folders using + Add Enabled Path
  3. Click Save Settings
  4. Click the Scan for Music button
  5. Wait for the scan process to build your library
Tip: Jamber3 supports MP3, FLAC, WAV, and other common audio formats. It will extract metadata from your files automatically.

Interface Overview

  • Toolbar: Contains Scan, Help, and Settings buttons
  • Left Pane: Music library browser with search and filters
  • Right Pane: Song details, resources, and playback controls

Scanning Music

Adding Music Folders

To add music folders to your library:

  1. Click the Settings ‚öôÔ∏è button
  2. In the Enabled Scan Paths section, click + Add Enabled Path
  3. Browse and select your music folder
  4. Click Add Path to confirm
  5. Click Save Settings

Excluding Folders

To exclude certain folders from scanning:

  1. In Settings, find the Excluded Paths section
  2. Click + Add Excluded Path
  3. Select folders you want to exclude (you can exclude sub-directories of an included Music folder, for example)
  4. These folders will be skipped during scanning

The Scan Process

The scan process:

  • Searches all enabled paths for music files
  • Extracts metadata (title, artist, album, etc.)
  • Builds a searchable database
  • Progress is shown in the status area
Note: Large music collections may take several minutes to scan initially. Subsequent scans are much faster as they only process new or changed files.

Browsing Your Library

View Modes

Choose how to organize your music library:

  • Artists: Organized by artist name with expandable albums
  • Albums: Grouped by album with track listings
  • Folders: Shows your music in its original folder structure
  • All: Simple list of all songs

Searching Songs

Use the search box at the top of the left pane:

  • Type artist names, song titles, or album names
  • Search is case-insensitive and searches as you type
  • Results update in real-time

Filtering Options

Additional filtering options:

  • Setlist Filter: Show only songs from a specific setlist
  • Resource Filter: Show only songs with guitar tabs, lyrics, or other resources

Setlist Management

Filtering by Setlists

Use the setlist dropdown in the left pane to show only songs from a specific setlist. This is great for:

  • Practicing a specific set
  • Reviewing songs for an upcoming performance
  • Organizing songs by difficulty or theme

Song Details & Resources

Viewing Song Details

Click any song to see detailed information in the right pane:

  • Basic Info: Title, artist, album, duration
  • File Info: Location, format, bitrate, file size
  • Resources: Guitar tabs, bass tabs, lyrics links
  • Setlist Membership: Which setlists contain this song

Editing Song Information

To edit song information:

  1. View the song details
  2. Click Edit Song
  3. Modify the title, artist, or album fields
  4. Click Save Changes

Finding Guitar Tabs & Lyrics

Jamber3 can help you find resources for learning songs:

Guitar Tabs:

  • Click Find Guitar Tab
  • Review search results from Ultimate Guitar and other sources
  • Click Accept to save the best result

Lyrics:

  • Click Find Lyrics
  • Review and select the most accurate lyrics
  • Lyrics will be stored and displayed in the song details
Tip: You can also manually add resource URLs by clicking Add Manual URL and pasting links you've found elsewhere.

Audio Playback

Control audio playback from the song details:

  • Play/Pause: Click the play button or use Spacebar
  • Volume: Use the volume slider
  • Seek: Click on the progress bar to jump to a specific position
  • Speed Control: Adjust playback speed from 0.5x to 2.0x for practice
  • Pitch Control: Adjust the pitch up or down for practice (requires compatible audio format)

Troubleshooting

Common Issues

Songs Not Appearing After Scan

  • Check that your music folders are added to "Enabled Paths" in Settings
  • Ensure folders aren't listed in "Excluded Paths"
  • Verify files are in supported formats (MP3, FLAC, WAV, etc.)
  • Try running a fresh scan

Search Not Working

  • Clear the search box and try again
  • Check if any filters are applied that might hide results
  • Verify songs exist by browsing in "All" view mode

Audio Playback Issues

  • Check that the audio file exists at its original location
  • Verify the file isn't corrupted by playing it in another audio player
  • Try adjusting the volume slider

Performance Tips

Performance Tips:
  • Exclude large non-music folders to speed up scanning
  • Keep your music collection organized in a reasonable folder structure
  • Regular scans are faster than initial scans
  • Close other resource-intensive applications during large scans
